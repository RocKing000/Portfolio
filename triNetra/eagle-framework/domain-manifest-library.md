# Domain Manifest Library

The Eagle Framework operates across 30 canonical high-risk domains organised into six super-domains. A domain-specific Input Manifest template exists for each domain.

---

## The Thirty Domains

### Infrastructure
Cybersecurity · Critical Infrastructure · Energy Systems · Telecommunications

### Economic
Financial Services · Supply Chain · Insurance · Real Estate and Built Environment · Capital Allocation

### Human Safety
Healthcare · Pharmaceuticals · Transportation · Aerospace · Robotics and Autonomous Systems

### Governance
Public Sector and Citizen Services · Legal and Regulatory Compliance · Enterprise Governance · Judicial Systems

### Intelligence
AI Infrastructure · Media and Information Systems · Research and Scientific Systems · Intelligence and Investigations Analysis

### Physical World
Defense · Workforce Systems · Education · Manufacturing · Biotechnology · Climate and Environmental Systems · Food and Agriculture · Emergency Management

---

## What a Domain Manifest Carries

Each domain-specific Input Manifest template carries:

- A full domain taxonomy covering decisions, evidence types, observers, and threat domains
- Utility bounds specific to the deployment context
- Epistemic priors calibrated to the domain's uncertainty characteristics
- Dataset splits for evaluation
- Human baselines for the domain
- Applicable regulatory frameworks

---

## Using a Domain Manifest

The assessed entity selects the template corresponding to their deployment domain and completes the Input Manifest with their system's specifics: decision space, structural characteristics, oversight configuration, and traceability posture.

If the system spans multiple domains or falls outside the 30-domain catalogue, a generic Input Manifest template is also available.

All domain templates and the generic template are available for download at [trinetra.life/cyber-eagle-docs](https://trinetra.life/cyber-eagle-docs).
