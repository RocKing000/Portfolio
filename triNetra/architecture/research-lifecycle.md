# Research Lifecycle

All triNetra engagements, whether a structured research commission or an Eagle Insight Platform (EIP) assessment, operate through the same six-stage lifecycle. The lifecycle is not a project management framework. It is the structural sequence that produces a valid research output under the triNetra Pattern Research Standard (TPRS).

---

## The Six Stages

### Stage 1: Input

The engagement begins with the client's current reality as observed, not as assumed by triNetra.

The client provides the system description, operational context, and the problem as they currently understand it. triNetra does not pre-qualify the problem or impose a diagnosis before input is received. The presenting problem may or may not be the actual structural problem. Stage 1 treats the client's description as the starting point, not the conclusion.

---

### Stage 2: Architecture

From the input, triNetra derives two architectural maps.

| Map | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| Approximate current state | What the system actually is, structurally, beneath the surface description |
| Ideal state | What the system should be, given the organisation's real constraints and direction |

The ideal state is not a generic best-practice model. It is derived from the organisation's own operational reality and constraints: what it is capable of, what it is regulated to do, and what direction it is already moving in.

---

### Stage 3: Gap Analysis

The comparison between the approximate current state and the ideal state reveals the gaps. Not all gaps are equal.

| Gap Type | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Structural | Prevents the system from functioning as intended under pressure |
| Operational | Creates friction but does not cause structural failure |
| Cosmetic | Affects presentation but not substance |

The Gap Analysis quantifies the delta and classifies each gap by structural consequence. The research prioritises structural gaps. Operational and cosmetic gaps are documented but do not drive the framework derivation.

---

### Stage 4: Framework

The framework is derived from the gap analysis. It is not derived from generic templates, industry standards, or external best practice. It addresses the specific structural gaps identified in Stage 3, in the order they matter.

The framework is designed to be governed independently by the organisation. It is a structural instrument the organisation owns, not a consulting deliverable that requires ongoing external interpretation.

---

### Stage 5: Delivery

The framework is delivered in a form comprehensible to the teams who will govern and evolve it.

| Engagement Type | Delivery Form |
|-----------------|---------------|
| EIP assessment | Assessment Report |
| Research engagement | Structural framework document and pattern study |

triNetra does not implement the framework. Delivery closes the engagement.

---

### Stage 6: Evolution

Every framework delivered under the TPRS is designed to evolve. Stage 6 is not a separate engagement. It is a design requirement built into Stage 4. The framework must be able to grow as the system grows without losing structural coherence.

For EIP clients, Evolution Analysis (optional) tracks structural progression across successive assessment inputs and maps the trajectory against the maturity level targets identified in the Assessment Report.
