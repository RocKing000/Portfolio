# Biotech Domain

**Status:** Active Research  
**Domain:** Biotechnology · AI-driven drug discovery · AI governance

---

## Research Question

What structural conditions must an AI system operating in biotechnology and AI-driven drug discovery satisfy to meet the Eagle Framework's auditability standard?

---

## What the Study Examines

The Biotech Domain study examines the structural intersection of three areas:

**Computational biology and AI-driven drug discovery.** AI systems are being deployed to accelerate target identification, molecule generation, and clinical trial design. These systems operate in a high-consequence domain: the outputs influence decisions that affect patient safety, drug approval, and regulatory compliance.

**High-consequence decision-making in clinical and regulatory environments.** Decisions made in the clinical and regulatory pipeline carry consequences for patient populations and public health systems. The Eagle Framework was designed for high-consequence decision-making systems. The Biotech Domain study tests whether the Framework's seven dimensions apply to this domain as specified, or whether the domain requires dimension adaptations.

**Eagle Framework applicability.** The study produces a domain-specific assessment of which Eagle Framework dimensions are most structurally consequential in the biotech and drug discovery context, and what modifications, if any, the domain requires.

---

## Why This Matters

The biotech and drug discovery domain is entering a period of rapid AI deployment without an established structural auditability standard. Regulators including the FDA, EMA, and equivalent bodies are developing guidance, but the structural assessment methodology for AI in this domain is not yet codified.

The Biotech Domain study contributes to that codification: a pattern-derived, evidence-grounded structural framework for AI auditability in biotech, produced through the same methodology as the Eagle Framework itself.
