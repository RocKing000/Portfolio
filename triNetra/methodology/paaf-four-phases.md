# PaaF: Four Phases

PaaF (Patterns as a Framework) operates through four phases. Each phase either resolves the current structural constraint or reveals a deeper one. The process continues until the system's governing structure becomes visible.

---

## Phase 1: Problem Identification Research

Research begins at the symptom layer and works downward through process, structure, and dependency until the source condition is located.

The question is not what is failing. The question is why it was always going to fail.

Most problems presented for structural analysis are proxies for deeper structural conditions. A system that produces inconsistent outputs has an inconsistency symptom. The structural condition producing that symptom may be an evidence attribution failure, a confidence miscalibration, or a dependency architecture that makes consistency impossible. Problem Identification Research locates the structural condition, not the symptom.

---

## Phase 2: Pattern and Framework Design

Patterns are derived from observed system reality, not from theory or best practice.

Recurring structures, dependency chains, and failure modes are extracted from what the system actually does. Not from what it is supposed to do. These patterns are translated into a governed, modular framework the organisation can own and maintain independently.

The framework is not a consulting deliverable that requires ongoing interpretation by an external party. It is a structural instrument that the organisation governs itself.

---

## Phase 3: Structural Interpretation

The framework is interpreted for the teams who will govern and evolve it.

Contradictions, survivability pressures, and trajectory direction are made explicit. Structural Interpretation answers the questions that the pattern analysis raises: what does this mean for how the system operates under pressure? Where does the framework predict failure? What survivability pressures will cause the system to deviate from its stated governance posture?

---

## Phase 4: Evolution Direction

Structural gaps are identified before they become failures. Adaptable pathways are designed for the framework to grow without losing structural coherence.

Evolution Direction is not a roadmap. It is a structural analysis of the conditions under which the current framework will become insufficient. It identifies what structural changes would be required to maintain governance integrity as the system evolves.

---

## The Recursive Property

PaaF is recursive because each phase can reveal that the previous phase was operating on an incorrect assumption about what the actual problem is. When that happens, the process returns to Phase 1 with a more precise problem statement. The recursion terminates when the structural condition is located with sufficient precision that a governed framework can be derived from it.
