# triNetra Pattern Research Standard (TPRS)

All triNetra research engagements are conducted under the triNetra Pattern Research Standard (TPRS). The TPRS governs the integrity of the research process from problem intake through to delivery.

---

## What the Standard Governs

**Evidence grounding.** All structural findings must be grounded in evidence derived from the system under study. Findings that cannot be traced to specific observed structural conditions are not valid under the TPRS.

**Pattern derivation.** Frameworks produced under the TPRS are derived from observed patterns in the system's actual behaviour, not from theoretical models or generic best practice. A framework that is not derived from the system it governs is not a TPRS-compliant deliverable.

**Independent execution.** triNetra conducts its research independently. The organisation or institution under study does not participate in pattern extraction, scoring, or framework derivation. It provides the input and receives the output.

**Structural diagnosis, not prescription.** The TPRS prohibits triNetra from producing implementation plans, vendor recommendations, technology roadmaps, or strategy documents. The deliverable is always a structural diagnosis: what the system is, where it fails, and what structural conditions need to change.

---

## What an Engagement Produces

Under the TPRS, a research engagement produces one or more of:

- A structural assessment (Organisation Intelligence, through the Eagle Framework)
- A comparative structural analysis (Alignment Intelligence or Change Analysis)
- A structural framework the organisation can own and govern independently
- A pattern study addressing a specific structural question

What it does not produce: implementation plans, vendor shortlists, technology roadmaps, or ongoing consulting relationships.

---

## What triNetra Does Not Do

triNetra does not implement systems. It does not recommend vendors. It does not produce strategy decks. It does not maintain ongoing management relationships with assessed organisations.

It derives the structural pattern beneath a problem and hands the organisation a governed framework it can maintain independently. This is not a limitation. It is the standard that makes the research credible.
